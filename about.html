An overnight success, 12-years in the making.

We started out as entrepreneurs. We never intended to become investors.

We started our first business in 2007, and before we knew it, we had hundreds of employees across a bunch of companies. Every once in a while, we'd get an email from a private equity firm. Their emails were confusing. They bragged about their AUM and CAGR and dropped that they'd gone to Harvard every third sentence.

They were aliens. We did not speak their language. Spreadsheet jockeys who had never met a payroll.

Eventually, we gave in and sold one of our businesses to them. It was a brutal six-month marathon. Piles of legal documents, constant renegotiations, and all-day meetings that spooked our management team.

We started Niedbala Companies to be different.

We decided to do it ourselves using Buffett's simple approach. We worked backwards from everything we hated about selling and built a process that is direct, respectful, and fast.

We make offers in days, not months, let founders dictate their role (stay, go, or something in between), and leave acquired companies alone for the long-term.

Life is too short to sell to a guy named Chad who calls your team "human capital".

Here's how we do it:

Speak plainly

Don't waste people's time. No buzzwords or finance theater.

Protect culture

Do not mess with the culture that makes each company unique.

Avoid synergy

Each business operates independently. Forced integration is out.

Make founders proud

We work hard to honor the original vision and build on it.

No assholes

Life is too short for brilliant jerks, narcissists, and ego games.

Leave people alone

Buy great companies, hire great leaders, and let them run.
